Corporation Tax Rates

Trading Rate of Corporation Tax

The trading rate of corporation tax is 12.5% and applies to the following income and gains: Schedule D, Case I and Case II profits; capital gains (as adjusted); and certain foreign dividends.

Passive Rate of Corporation Tax

The passive rate of corporation tax is 25% and applies to: Case III income (foreign income and untaxed Irish interest, excluding certain foreign dividends taxable at 12.5%); Case IV income (taxed Irish income and miscellaneous income); Case V income (Irish rental income); and income from an "excepted trade".

CAP 1 — Formulae

Capital asset pricing model

rj = rf + β(rm - rf)

Dividend valuation model (no growth – finding the cost of equity)

Ke = D1 / P0

Dividend valuation model (with growth – finding the cost of equity)

Ke = D0(1 + g) / P0 + g

Economic order quantity model

EOQ = √(2FU / CP)

Growth in dividends

g = (Dt0 / Dt0-y)1/y - 1

Growth (using retentions)

g = br

Interpolation (IRR)

IRR = Rate 1 + [NPV 1 / (NPV 1 - NPV 2)] × (Rate 2 – Rate 1)

Irredeemable debt (cost of debt)

Kd = i(1-t) / D

Nominal discount rate

Nominal rate = ((1 + Real rate) × (1 + Inflation rate)) - 1

Weighted average cost of capital (WACC)

WACC = E(Ke) / (D + E) + D(Kd(1-t)) / (D + E)

Annualised cost of discount

d / (100 - d) × 365 / (reduction in receivables days)
